Build the telemetry aggregation layer that consumes normalized epoch records and computes deterministic time-series buckets, trend inputs, expected-tool gap rates, and derived efficiency/cost metrics. Keep this task scoped to aggregation and formulas; reader normalization is 079.005-T and report slicing/rendering is 079.012-T.

<!-- BEGIN:acceptance-criteria -->
- Aggregation tests cover date/range buckets over normalized epoch records from 079.005-T without reading sinks directly.
- Per-tool gap tests assert `missing_expected_tool_counts[tool] = max(expected_tool_counts[tool] - observed_tool_counts[tool], 0)` and scalar totals remain equal to their per-tool map sums.
- Derived metrics are deterministic: `net_offload_tokens = avoided_read_estimated_tokens - tool_output_estimated_tokens`, `consumption_generation_ratio = input_tokens / output_tokens`, `gap_rate`, and `cost_per_successful_epoch`.
- Range and bucket derived ratios are computed from aggregate totals by summing numerators and denominators across epochs, then dividing; reports must not average per-epoch ratios.
- A successful epoch is defined from `AbsoluteOutcome` as having at least one recorded gate exit code, all recorded gate exit codes equal to `0`, and not `blocked`; empty gate-outcome collections are not successful. `cost_per_successful_epoch = sum(cogs_usd over successful epochs) / count(successful epochs)`.
- Any derived metric reports `unavailable` — never `0` and never an exception — when any operand is `unavailable`/null or its denominator is zero, including zero successful epochs or `output_tokens == 0`.
<!-- END:acceptance-criteria -->
