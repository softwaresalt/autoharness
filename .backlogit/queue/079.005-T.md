title: Building autoharness time-series telemetry reports

Add an autoharness-owned epoch-level reporting library that reads local telemetry sinks and produces deterministic time-series summaries for trends, regressions, cost/usage efficiency, offload, and expected-tool gap detection. Reports operate on persisted epoch fields, not a queryable event store.

<!-- BEGIN:acceptance-criteria -->
- Reports can summarize a selected date/range or task/session/phase/backlog-item slice from local SQLite and/or JSONL epoch data under `.autoharness/metrics/`; every slice key must be backed by a persisted `ExecutionEpoch` v1.1 field such as `session_id`, `task_id`, `backlog_item_id`, `phase`, `branch`, or `commit_sha`.
- Trend output includes token consumption, token generation, context-area estimates, COGS, duration, routed-vs-raw usage, avoided-read evidence, expected counts, observed counts, missing counts, and per-tool gap rates over time.
- Efficiency/cost-effectiveness metrics are deterministic: `net_offload_tokens = avoided_read_estimated_tokens - tool_output_estimated_tokens`, `consumption_generation_ratio = input_tokens / max(output_tokens, 1)`, `gap_rate`, and `cost_per_successful_epoch` or another documented denominator.
- Output distinguishes observed, estimated, derived, and unavailable measurements.
- The CLI/library degrades gracefully when telemetry is disabled, the DB is absent, or only partial v1.0 records exist.
<!-- END:acceptance-criteria -->

<!-- BEGIN:implementation-notes -->
Autoharness owns this local epoch time-series reporting surface. Backlogit may link to reports later but should not become the metric store. Do not require an external service or event table. If a CLI subcommand would push the task over the 2-hour bound, leave CLI wiring to a follow-up task and complete the reporting library first.
<!-- END:implementation-notes -->
